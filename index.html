About

The 2020 IEEE MIT Undergraduate Research Technology Conference (URTC) brings together undergraduates from around the world to present, discuss, and develop solutions to advance technology for humanity. Students may publish papers of their school projects, research, innovations, or case studies. Conference attendees will attend a rich program with keynote speeches and technical talks featuring renowned speakers, a student design competition, and networking events. The 2020 URTC conference will be hosted virtually.

Leslie is a Professor at MIT. She has an undergraduate degree in Philosophy and a PhD in Computer Science from Stanford, and was previously on the faculty at Brown University. She was the founding editor-in-chief of the Journal of Machine Learning Research. Her goal is to make intelligent robots, although she is not, herself, a robot.

Leslie Kaelbling
Professor of Computer Science and Engineering, MIT

Sarah Maston is a senior solution architect at Microsoft. She works with our partner ecosystem to build global scalable IoT solutions on Azure. She specializes in process architecture and data solution architecture. She is the founder of Project 15 from Microsoft (http://aka.ms/Project15) and the inventor of the Project Edison, a safety notification and response 3rd party solution accelerator for safe schools and workplaces, for which she won the Microsoft Polaris Award. In her 20 year career, she has architected many of the data warehouses out in the world including those at Monster.com, CouchSurfing.com, and Boston Medical Center. Prior to joining Microsoft, she won an Excellence & Eminence Award at IBM for the invention of the Nutrition Graph and an Outstanding Innovation Award for the invention of Simple Data Pipe.

Sarah Maston
IoT Solution Architect, Microsoft

Daisuke Nakahara is IoT Architect of Microsoft's Worldwide Internet of Things and Mixed Reality Sales team. In this role, Daisuke is responsible for technical engagements with partners to build IoT solutions. With 15+ years of experience as a Windows developer, Daisuke Nakahara focuses on building IoT solutions from the device point of view. His expertise includes architecting and designing IoT solutions, building devices, writing device code, interacting with cloud backend, and running workloads on edge devices. With emergence of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning technology, Daisuke also focuses on implementing AI and ML in both cloud and edge devices.

Daisuke Nakahara
IoT Solution Architect, Microsoft
